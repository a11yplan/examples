WCAG 1.3.3: Sensory Characteristics

Success Criterion 1.3.3 Sensory Characteristics (Level A)

Requirement: Instructions provided for understanding and operating content do not rely solely on sensory characteristics of components such as shape, color, size, visual location, orientation, or sound.

Purpose: Ensures that users who cannot perceive color, shape, size, or visual location can still understand instructions. This is critical for users who are blind, have low vision, are colorblind, or use screen readers.

Common Issues: Instructions like "Click the red button", "Select the round icon", "Use the button on the right", or "Press the large button" without additional text identification.

Testing Guidelines

Manual Testing:

  • Read all instructions without looking at the interface - can you identify the correct control?
  • Use a screen reader to navigate - are instructions meaningful without visual context?
  • Test with color blindness simulators - can you still follow instructions?
  • Resize the page or change orientation - do position-based instructions still work?

Automated Testing: This criterion is difficult to test automatically. Scanners can flag potential issues (e.g., instructions containing words like "red", "right", "round", "large") but human review is required to determine if text alternatives are provided.
