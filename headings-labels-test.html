Detection Method

Automated detection can identify potential issues:

  • Headings with generic patterns (Section, Part, Chapter + number only)
  • Labels with vague text ("Enter", "Choose", "Select", "Input", "Text")
  • Single-word or very short headings without context
  • Repeated identical headings on same page
  • Fieldset legends that are generic ("Options", "Settings", "Choices")

Common Generic Heading Patterns (to Avoid)

  • "Section 1", "Section 2", "Part A", "Part B"
  • "Information", "Details", "Content"
  • "Introduction", "Overview" (without context)
  • Numbers or letters alone (1, 2, 3, or A, B, C)
  • "More", "Other", "Additional"

Common Vague Label Patterns (to Avoid)

  • "Enter text", "Type here", "Input"
  • "Choose", "Select", "Pick one"
  • "Field 1", "Field 2", "Data"
  • "Information", "Details", "Value"

Best Practices

Good headings should:

  • Clearly describe the content of the section
  • Be unique or contextually distinct
  • Use meaningful words, not just numbers
  • Provide sufficient context for understanding
  • Help users scan and navigate page structure

Good labels should:

  • Clearly indicate what information is expected
  • Be specific about the data type or purpose
  • Avoid generic verbs without context
  • Help users understand form requirements
  • Include units or format when relevant (e.g., "Age (years)")

Note

While this criterion requires descriptive headings and labels, context matters. A heading might be acceptable if surrounding content provides clarity. However, headings and labels should be understandable on their own when possible, especially for users who navigate by headings or form fields.
